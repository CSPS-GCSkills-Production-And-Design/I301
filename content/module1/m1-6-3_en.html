Transfer of Knowledge and Information

Every year, thousands of government employees retire and thousands of new employees join the public service. The proper management of information is critical when it comes to knowledge and information transfer.

At one point or another in your career you will join a new team, start a new project, leave on an assignment, or retire. How do you ensure that knowledge and information are preserved?

Click on each of the tabs below to learn what you can do and when.

  • Starting a job
    • When starting a new job, always familiarize yourself with the designated corporate repository and know where you should file your information.
    • If certain IM procedures are new to you, talk to your manager and request formal training.
    • Meet with your IM professionals. They will provide you with useful information to get you started.
  • During a job
    • Follow IM guidelines and best practices in your daily routine. Take note of any instructions or messages you receive regarding IM updates or new initiatives.
    • Organize and file information according to the standards and procedures established by your organization.
    • Do not wait till the last minute to file your information. Do it on a regular basis to save time when managing a project.
    • Adopt an open-by-default attitude towards information. If the information does not contain any personal or sensitive data, make it available to the largest audience possible.
  • Leaving a job
    • Follow the internal policies for the administrative closure of your business processes and discuss your responsibilities with your manager when leaving a job.
    • Provide pertinent information about everything you leave to your successor, explaining why it will be needed.
    • Organize and file information of business value accordingly.
    • Properly destroy or delete all transitory information (Information that is required only for a limited time to ensure the completion of a routine action or the preparation of a subsequent record. It does not include records required by government institutions or ministers to control, support or document the delivery of programs; to carry out operations; to make decisions; or to account for activities of government).
