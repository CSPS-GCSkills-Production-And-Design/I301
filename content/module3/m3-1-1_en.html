Modern-Day Communication

Nowadays, electronic messaging such as emails and instant messages are the predominant mode of communication for most people.

Email is one of the most common forms of internal and external communication in the Government of Canada. (By "email", we mean both individual email and generic email accounts.)

Did you know?

Library and Archives Canada issued Email Management Guidelines for the public service in 2008. At that time, it was estimated that on average:

  • Government employees each received and created at least 50 emails every day; and
  • Government organizations received or sent 50 million email messages every year.

Not including instant messaging, we can definitely state that these numbers have increased significantly over the past few years.
