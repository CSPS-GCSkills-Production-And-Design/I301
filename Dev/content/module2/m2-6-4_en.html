Organizational Guidelines

The rationale behind having IM business rules in your organization is so that every employee uses the same information management procedures.

Within your organization's IM guidelines, you should be able to find the following sections.

Click on each section to find out more.

  • Roles and responsibilities
    • This section will identify your roles and responsibilities with respect to the business rules of your organization. It will clearly state how you should manage your information. You will also find the description of the roles of the system administrator and key players like your manager and IM specialists.
  • General naming conventions
    • This section will guide you on how to properly name your information so that everyone in your organization can easily search for and find it. If everyone were to name their documents the way they think they should be named, we would end up with a multitude of information in our databases which could be very difficult and time-consuming to find.
  • Using set of predefined metadata
    • There are different types of metadata you may need to add to your documents. The business rules will guide you on capturing the proper metadata for the type of information you are saving.
  • Directions for version control management
    • This section will explain the vision of your organization for version control. There may be different ways to manage these versions depending on the system you use. Here, you will learn the details on how to create versions of your documents.
  • Controlling access to information
    • This section will provide information to ensure that the proper IM team has sufficient access to your documents and folders. Although the Government of Canada is going with an open concept for sharing information, there is still a need to control some access to certain documents. This will ensure that more secure documents are accessible only to those who need access and who have the proper permissions to do so.
