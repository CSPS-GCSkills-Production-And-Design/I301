What Is Information of Enduring Value?

There is a special subgroup of information resources within information that has business value. It's called information resources of enduring value (IREV). These resources are also known as resources of historical or archival value.

IREVs are information resources that have long-term importance and relevance to Canadian society. Examples include research documents that contain new or revolutionary ideas expected to be useful in the future, or legal documents that detail a court decision that may dramatically change how other courts view this issue.

The volume of IREVs identified by your organization depends on its mandate and not the size. For example, the Privy Council Office, because of its mandate, will produce a larger percentage of these resources than other larger organizations.
