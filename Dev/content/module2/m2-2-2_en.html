Identifying Information of Business Value

How do you identify an information resource that has business value?

Every day, in your personal life, you identify information resources of business value at home, using the same principle that you would use at work.

To help you understand this concept better, let's take a look at your personal information resources that have value.

Here are a few items you might find in your mailbox. Which of the following items would you consider an IBV for you?

Hydro bill

Birth certificate

Eye doctor leaflet

Grocery flyer

Canada Revenue Agency’s Notice of Assessment


Right answer.

Wrong answer.

Hydro bill Business value The bill is a confirmation of your monthly amount owed. You will need to keep it for a long period.
Birth certificate Business value You need to keep your birth certificate indefinitely as proof of your birth and your identity.
Eye doctor leaflet No business value Probably no need to keep this. Even if you make an appointment with this doctor, you can recycle it later.
Grocery flyer No business value You may look at it and take note of what you want to buy, after which you will recycle it.
Canada Revenue Agency’s Notice of Assessment Business value The Notice of Assessment from the Canada Revenue Agency notifies you of the amount of tax you owe and the amount of tax you already paid. You will need to keep it for at least six years.
