Examples of Transitory Information

Here are some examples of transitory information that you might find in your work context:

  • A working draft of a project plan that was never formally circulated;
  • A copy of your letter of offer that an administrative assistant kept;
  • Emails you received as part of a distribution list or for information;
  • Meeting requests or calendar invitations;
  • An email which you were copied on and which the originator has already saved;
  • A copy of your section's organizational chart that you keep for ease of reference;
  • Casual emails or instant messages such as invitations to lunch or thank‑you notes; and
  • A voicemail message sent by your manager last week informing you that she was sick.

Remember, copies that you keep for reference purposes – or just in case you need them – are not considered information resources of business value, they are transitory. In fact, all copies are transitory.

Did you know?

Depending on your organization, transitory information represents approximately 75% to 80% of your total information resources.
