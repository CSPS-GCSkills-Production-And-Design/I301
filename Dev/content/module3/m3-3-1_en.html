Email Management Dos and Don'ts

It is good practice to establish a routine for handling your emails on a daily, weekly, and monthly basis. This will allow you to have better control over your emails and your time.

Below are a few dos and don'ts of email management.

Manage your email regularly

Don't be one of those employees who has several thousand emails stored in their email system

Use a signature block in all of your emails, including replies

Don't add personalized taglines under your signature

Save your emails of business value in your designated corporate repository

Don't create "archival" folders in Outlook (or any other email system) to store emails

Delete any transitory emails that are no longer necessary

Don't wait until your organization or IM professionals advise you to clean up your emails

In an email discussion, if you initiated the conversation, only keep the first and last email so you can see the email thread

Don't save every email "just in case" or because you "might need it one day"

Treat your email as government property

Don't delete emails that are subject to access to information and privacy requests
