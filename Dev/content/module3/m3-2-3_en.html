Who Should File Electronic Information of Business Value?

Email or instant messages that have business value should be filed only once in a corporate repository.

Who should file these messages? It depends on several factors.

Internal messages External messages
  • If you send an email or instant message that reflects a decision or an initiated action that is your responsibility, you are responsible for ensuring the message gets saved in a corporate repository.
  • If you receive an email of business value from an external source and you are the only recipient, you are responsible for ensuring it gets saved in a corporate repository.
  • If you are part of a project team, the person responsible for the project or a business process should ensure that messages of business value are saved in a corporate repository.
  • If an email of business value from an external source is sent to multiple persons in your organization, it is the responsibility of the project lead or the person responsible for the business process to ensure it gets saved in a corporate repository.
Note that the project lead or the person responsible for the business process can delegate the responsibility of saving emails or instant messages to another person.

 

When filing electronic messages, you should always follow the guidelines and best practices of your organization.
