Email Management Best Practices

In general, your organization or your business unit should have best practices to guide you on how to better manage your electronic messages.

Click on the sections below to see a few practical examples.

  • Using links or paths to files rather than attachments whenever possible
    • Attachments fill up mailboxes and cause multiple copies of documents to circulate. Instead, point to the file location or intranet location of the document.
  • Sending only one email per subject
    • One topic and a brief message make sorting and filing easy.
  • Making email subject lines meaningful and specific
    • A meaningful subject line provides enough information for the recipient to decide on its importance and makes it easy to retrieve.
    • You might want to agree on a naming convention for the subject line of your email within your business unit or organization.
  • Being mindful when choosing recipients to help colleagues manage their inboxes
    • Use the "To:" field if you expect a response from the recipient.
    • Use the "Cc:" field only for those who need the context.
    • Limit the use of "Reply all".
