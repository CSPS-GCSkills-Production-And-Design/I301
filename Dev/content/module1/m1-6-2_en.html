IM Roles and Responsibilities: Managers

Managers, IM functional specialists, and IM senior officials are there to help you manage your information. Here is what you can do if you have questions or need help:

  • Discuss your IM needs with your manager (for example, if you need more time to clean up your information); and
  • Consult with your IM specialist to get additional help on IM‑related matters (for example, access to specific folders).

Do you want to know more about how managers and IM specialists can help you as an employee?

Click on the tabs below to learn about the IM responsibilities of each of the following groups.

  • Managers
    • Ensure that employees are aware of their IM responsibilities and have all the tools and necessary training.
    • Ensure that IM requirements are included in the contractual terms and conditions for those engaged in work on behalf of the Government of Canada.
    • Identify information issues to IM specialists and address them as soon as possible.
    • Report any IM‑related issues that cannot be resolved by an IM specialist to IM senior officials.
  • IM functional specialists
    • Provide advice and guidance to employees on all IM‑related matters.
    • Develop and deliver IM training and awareness sessions to personnel at all levels.
    • Meet with employees and managers to discuss their IM needs.
  • IM senior officials
    • Co‑ordinate, promote, and direct information management within the organization.
    • Co‑ordinate IM resources and the implementation of related activities in the organization.
    • Ensure IM requirements are addressed as part of organizational strategic planning.
    • Adopt government-wide IM solutions within their respective organizations.
