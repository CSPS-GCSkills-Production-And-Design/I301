Open Government

In the Government of Canada, open government is about fostering greater openness and accountability, enhancing citizen participation in policymaking and service design, and creating a more efficient and responsive government.

The international open government movement is led by the Open Government Partnership (OGP), a multilateral initiative that aims to secure concrete commitments from governments to promote transparency, empower citizens, fight corruption, and harness new technologies to strengthen governance.

Did you know?

OGP was formally launched in 2011 by 8 founding governments. Since that time, it has welcomed the commitment of 78 countries and a growing number of local governments to join the Partnership. Canada joined the OGP in 2012 and has since developed an open government plan every two years.

 

The OGP develops yearly implementation plans outlining a set of collective deliverables for its members. To meet these deliverables, the GC develops biennial National Action Plans on Open Government. A great deal of progress has been made in the years since its implementation in 2012, making the Government of Canada a leader in open government. To learn more about the current national action plan and its commitments to advance openness, transparency and accountability, see the Open Government webpage.
