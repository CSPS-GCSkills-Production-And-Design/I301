Benefits of Managing Information

Do you recall the last time you had to take over a colleague's work file or join a new project? Was it frustrating or time-consuming to look for the relevant information? Managing information properly has great benefits—not just for you, but for your organization and, indeed, all Canadians.

Click on the images below to learn more about the benefits of properly managing information.

Benefits for you

Benefits for you

For you, following IM best practices in your daily activities means:

  • Having easy and simple access to the key information that you need;
  • Saving time trying to find information to do your work;
  • Improving your day-to-day efficiency; and
  • Minimizing stress that may be associated with a job change.

Benefits for your organization

Benefits for your organization

For your organization, IM guidelines and best practices will help:

  • Eliminate the duplication of work for you and your colleagues (why re‑invent the wheel?);
  • Support informed decision making at all levels;
  • Produce performance and project reports efficiently; and
  • Reduce litigation costs significantly.
